# Split Lease Host Call Analysis: Complete Picture

**Date:** November 28, 2025
**Source:** 47 host call transcripts from Craigslist outreach
**Method:** Batch-based qualitative analysis with quantitative validation
**Purpose:** Understand host psychology, conversion drivers, blockers, and strategic opportunities

---

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 47 host calls reveals a market more receptive than initially perceived. The majority (87.2%) remain open to Split Lease, with only 6 hosts declining outright. The key findings span across host segmentation, conversion patterns, concerns that need addressing, and clear disqualification signals.

**Top-Line Numbers:**
- 53.2% likely to convert
- 34.0% conditional (convertible with right approach)
- 12.8% declined
- 100% of platform-resistant hosts decline

## 1. Who Are These Hosts?

### Three Distinct Segments

| Segment | Count | % | Who They Are |
|---------|-------|---|--------------|
| **Owner-Occupant** | 28 | 59.6% | Lives in the space, renting room or time-share |
| **Investor** | 11 | 23.4% | Multiple properties, professional landlord mindset |
| **Other** | 8 | 17.0% | Single property they don't live in |

---

### Owner-Occupants (28 hosts)

The majority segment. They live in or with the property.

**Characteristics:**
- Personal stake in who enters their space
- Mixed on flexibility (some very flexible, some rigid)
- 35.7% prefer single guest arrangements
- 35.7% want long-term (3+ month) commitments
- Highest conversion rate: 60.7% likely

**The 28 hosts:**
> Chantel, Cristina, Dana, Deedra, Dimitri, Gale Grant, Gregory Stanford, Hemeden, Joie Coelho, Joie & James, Jonathan Kim, Keah, Kent, Kris, Lilly, Louise, Luci Chambers, Malvern, Nakoley Renville, Nneka, Patricia, Robbie, Scott White, Sherene, Stephanie, Tag, Toni, Zein

---

### Investors (11 hosts)

The professional segment. Property is a business.

**Characteristics:**
- Economics-driven decisions
- More flexible on arrangement types
- Zero rejections of any model offered
- Want guarantees and minimum income
- 54.5% likely conversion (not 30% as previously thought)

**The 11 hosts:**
> Andreas, David, Diane Kaufhold, Jessica Filomeno, John, June, Paul, Rami, Ramsey, Shirley, Tammy

**Scaling opportunity:** Tammy has 9 NYC units, Diane has multiple buildings, Ramsey has 3+ properties.

---

### Other (8 hosts)

The unclear segment. Single property, doesn't live there.

**Characteristics:**
- Often inherited or secondary property
- Less defined preferences
- Lower engagement level in calls
- 40.0% likely conversion (lowest)

**The 8 hosts:**
> Anthony, Drew, Herbert, Jason, Kelly, Ken Chang, Mahesh, Michael Spear

## 3. What Hosts Worry About

### Concern Distribution

| Concern | Count | % of Sample | Impact on Conversion |
|---------|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Trust/Vetting** | 14 | 29.8% | Moderate - addressable |
| **Payment Timing** | 11 | 23.4% | Moderate - addressable |
| **Platform Resistance** | 4 | 8.5% | Fatal - 100% decline |

---

### Trust Concerns (14 hosts)

These hosts worry about who they're letting into their space.

**Quote - Patricia:**
> "I've been scammed twice."

**Quote - Tag:**
> "I don't know if this guy is legit... could be sketchy, could be a scam."

**Quote - Paul:**
> "I don't even know if you're licensed."

**The 14 hosts with trust concerns:**
> Dimitri, Hemeden, Jessica Filomeno, June, Kent, Kris, Lilly, Louise, Mahesh, Michael Spear, Patricia, Paul, Tag

**Resolution:** Emphasize vetting process, show credentials, provide references.

**Conversion impact:** 8 of 14 (57%) still likely or conditional. Trust concerns are not fatal.

---

### Payment Concerns (11 hosts)

These hosts worry about getting paid reliably.

**Quote - Tammy:**
> "I don't understand that I can make what I need on a monthly basis."

**Quote - Michael Spear:**
> "How do I know you're gonna do that?"

**Quote - Robbie:**
> "We have to confirm the payment immediately."

**The 11 hosts with payment concerns:**
> Cristina, Hemeden, John, Kent, Michael Spear, Nneka, Patricia, Ramsey, Robbie, Sherene, Tammy

**Resolution:** Clear payment terms upfront, guaranteed deposit structure, monthly payment clarity.

**Conversion impact:** 9 of 11 (82%) still likely or conditional. Payment concerns are addressable.

---

### Platform Resistance (4 hosts) - THE DEAL KILLER

These hosts don't want to use any platform.

**Quote - Malvern:**
> "I don't want it put on some other website."

**Quote - Paul:**
> "This is how I work... cashier's check, my process."

**Quote - Kelly:**
> "I'd prefer to work with individuals directly."

**The 4 platform-resistant hosts:**
> Gregory Stanford, Kelly, Malvern, Paul

**Conversion impact:** 4 of 4 (100%) declined. Platform resistance is fatal.

## 9. Strategic Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Early Disqualification Screening

**Finding:** Platform resistance = 100% decline rate.

**Action:** Add first-contact question:
> "Do you prefer to handle rentals directly without using a platform?"

**Impact:** Saves sales effort on 8.5% of leads that will never convert.

**Disqualify immediately:** Gregory Stanford, Kelly, Malvern, Paul

---

### Recommendation 2: Address Payment Concerns Proactively

**Finding:** 23.4% have payment timing concerns, but 82% still convert.

**Action:** Lead with payment structure clarity:
- When first payment happens
- Monthly payment schedule
- Deposit handling

**Target hosts:** Cristina, Hemeden, John, Kent, Michael Spear, Nneka, Patricia, Ramsey, Robbie, Sherene, Tammy

---

### Recommendation 3: Build Trust Through Credentials

**Finding:** 29.8% have trust/vetting concerns, but 57% still convert.

**Action:**
- Mention licensing/credentials early
- Provide guest vetting process details
- Offer host references

**Target hosts:** Dimitri, Hemeden, Jessica Filomeno, June, Kent, Kris, Lilly, Louise, Mahesh, Michael Spear, Patricia, Paul, Tag

---

### Recommendation 4: Create Long-Term Product Tier

**Finding:** 25.5% want 3+ month commitments, concentrated in OO segment.

**Action:** Offer "Extended Stay" product:
- 3-6 month minimum
- Same guest throughout
- Simplified pricing (monthly rate)

**Target hosts:** Chantel, Drew, Jessica Filomeno, Kent, Kris, Lilly, Luci Chambers, Nakoley Renville, Scott White, Stephanie, Toni, Zein

---

### Recommendation 5: Develop Investor Partnership Program

**Finding:** Investors have 54.5% conversion rate, zero rejections of flexible models, and scale potential.

**Action:** Create building-level partnership:
- Guaranteed minimum income
- Multiple unit commitment
- Full-service management

**Priority investors:**
- Tammy (9 units)
- Diane Kaufhold (multiple buildings)
- Ramsey (3+ properties)
- Shirley (multiple Brooklyn units)

---

### Recommendation 6: Explore the "Untested" 66%

**Finding:** 31 hosts (66%) never fully heard the product options.

**Action:** Re-contact with complete offering:
- Present multiple arrangement types
- Don't assume single-guest preference
- Test flexibility

**Untested hosts with Likely conversion:**
> Andreas, Anthony, Cristina, Dimitri, Drew, Herbert, Jason, Keah, Ken Chang, Lilly, Nakoley Renville, Nneka, Rami, Shirley, Toni

## Conclusion: The Complete Picture

### Market Segmentation

| Segment | Size | Conversion | Best For |
|---------|------|------------|----------|
| Owner-Occupant | 59.6% | 60.7% | Single guest, long-term, personal relationship |
| Investor | 23.4% | 54.5% | Flexible arrangements, building partnerships |
| Other | 17.0% | 50.0% | Case-by-case evaluation |

### Conversion Drivers

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| No platform resistance | Required |
| No building restrictions | Important |
| Clear payment terms | Helpful |
| Trust/credentials shown | Helpful |

### Product Opportunities

| Product | Target Size | Key Hosts |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Long-term (3-6 month) | 25.5% | Scott White, Stephanie, Kris, Lilly |
| Single guest | 21.3% | Joie Coelho, Chantel, Dana |
| Flexible/rotating | 23.4% | Zein, Jonathan Kim, Diane Kaufhold |
| Investor partnership | 8.5% | Tammy, Diane, Ramsey |

### Immediate Actions

1. **Disqualify** platform resisters early (4 hosts)
2. **Address** payment concerns proactively (11 hosts)
3. **Build** trust with credentials (14 hosts)
4. **Create** long-term product tier (12 hosts)
5. **Develop** investor partnerships (4 hosts with scale)
6. **Re-contact** untested hosts with full offering (31 hosts)
