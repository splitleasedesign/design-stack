The Core Discovery
The platform was designed for and resonates with owner-occupants who use their space part-time (the "ghost roommate" scenario). However, the Craigslist outreach primarily attracted professional landlords with investment properties who have fundamentally different needs.
Key Finding
Hosts will accept more work and mental burden for more money, but only within specific constraints. The threshold isn't universal — it depends on the host's relationship to the space.

04
Money vs. Work Tradeoff
⇌
The Core Question
Will hosts accept more work for more money?

Answer: Yes, BUT with critical constraints.
Constraint 1: "Worth My Time"
Different host types have different thresholds. Investor hosts need $5K+/month. Owner-occupants are satisfied with $300-600/month but reject complexity.
Constraint 2: Mental Overhead Limit
Owner-occupants have a hard limit on mental overhead that isn't about money. Some experiences are unacceptable at any price.
Constraint 3: Relationship Quality
Some hosts will take LESS money for a better relationship. The equation isn't purely financial.

Strategic Recommendations
⇌
Lead with "One Guest, Long-Term"
Current pitch: Ghost roommates, split schedules, two guests
Recommended: "Find ONE reliable professional who needs your space exactly when you don't use it. Same person, every week, for months."
Refine Target Segment
Stop targeting investor hosts via Craigslist. Focus on owner-occupants with part-time usage patterns: consultants, flight attendants, divorced parents, remote workers with multi-city lives.
Build Co-Host Service
Multiple hosts explicitly asked for full-service management. Offer tiers: Self-Service, Assisted (SL creates listing), Full-Service (SL handles everything). Cristina: "If you can do the work for me..."
Address Mental Burden Proactively
In sales calls, address upfront: "You'll have ONE guest, not rotating strangers. They clean before leaving. You meet them on Zoom first. Maximum 4-week trial. Payment 24h after arrival."
Implement Trial Night Offering
Split Lease covers cleaning cost. Guest sends proposal with intended full stay. One trial night. If successful, full booking proceeds. Multiple hosts responded positively.
Reserve Ghost Roommates for Proven Hosts
Ghost roommate model should be a premium upsell for hosts who've proven comfortable with single guests — not the default offering for new hosts.
⇌
The Path Forward
The platform's legal architecture is sound. The challenge is matching that architecture to hosts who actually want what it offers. Target owner-occupants with single, long-term guests (not ghost roommates). Build co-host service for delegators. Reserve ghost roommate model for proven hosts. Pursue building-level partnerships with investor hosts if unit economics work.
