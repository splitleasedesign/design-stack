## 2. Civilization and Its Discontents
**Author:** Sigmund Freud

### Description
A seminal work exploring the fundamental tension between civilization and the individual. Freud argues that civilization's demands for conformity and instinctual repression are at odds with human desires for freedom, creating perpetual discontent. Originally published in 1930.

### Chapters
- **Chapter I** - The "oceanic" feeling as the source of religious energy
- **Chapter II** - Religion vs. arts and sciences; pleasure as the absence of pain
- **Chapter III** - Sources of unhappiness: nature, the body, and human relationships
- **Chapter IV** - Two types of love (sexual and family); developmental history of civilization
- **Chapter V** - Tensions between individual instincts and civilization's demands
- **Chapter VI** - Aggression and the death instinct
- **Chapter VII** - Continued analysis of instincts vs. society
- **Chapter VIII** - Societies evolving like children; civilization producing guilt; Eros vs. Thanatos

## 14. The Wisdom of Crowds
**Author:** James Surowiecki

### Description
A compelling exploration of how collective intelligence often outperforms individual expertise. Surowiecki demonstrates that under the right circumstances, groups are remarkably intelligent—often smarter than the smartest people in them.

### Chapters
1. **The Wisdom of Crowds** - Introduction to collective intelligence
2. **The Difference Difference Makes: Waggle Dances, the Bay of Pigs, and the Value of Diversity** - Why diversity matters
3. **Monkey See, Monkey Do: Imitation, Information Cascades, and Independence** - The dangers of herding
4. **Putting the Pieces Together: The CIA, Linux, and the Art of Decentralization** - Aggregating knowledge
5. **Shall We Dance?** - Coordination problems

**Key Concepts:**
- Conditions for crowd wisdom: diversity, independence, decentralization, aggregation
- When crowds fail: information cascades, groupthink
- Applications in markets, democracy, and organizations
