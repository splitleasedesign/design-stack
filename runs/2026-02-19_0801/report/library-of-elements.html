Three-Tier Button System

ds-ui-0801-001

A systematic button hierarchy with three visual tiers: Primary (solid gradient for binding actions like Accept Terms), Secondary (outline for non-binding actions like Send Interest), and Tertiary (text-link style for exploratory actions like Learn More). Ensures the guest instantly understands the commitment level of each action.

Implements: works-001, works-003, looks-001, feels-002, behaves-003
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: Primary buttons trigger full confirmation modals (acceptance stage only). Secondary buttons show inline confirmation with no modal. Tertiary links navigate without confirmation. Button state transitions use ease-out at 200ms.

Emotion: Primary: gravity and commitment. Secondary: invitation and safety. Tertiary: curiosity and exploration. The visual weight gradient mirrors the emotional weight of each action.

Test: First-click test: 70%+ of users identify the primary action within 5 seconds. A/B test: 20%+ increase in proposal creation with secondary styling vs. primary styling for the proposal button.

Cleaning Protocol Card

ds-ui-0801-002

A prominent, standalone content block on the listing page that proactively addresses cleanliness concerns. Shows three key facts (separate linens, cleaning responsibilities, photo verification) with icons. Positioned above the fold, after photos, before pricing. Resolves the #1 guest objection about shared living hygiene.

Implements: works-002, communicates-001, looks-002, feels-001
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: Card appears with a subtle fade-in (200ms ease-out) when it enters the viewport during scroll. No interaction required to view all three items. The 'View full cleaning standards' link opens an expandable detail section, not a new page.

Emotion: Safety and reassurance. The card resolves the guest's deepest anxiety (hygiene) with specific, sensory language and visual proof references. The purple-tinted icons and clean layout signal 'we take this seriously.'

Test: A/B test: 15%+ improvement in listing-to-proposal conversion when this card is visible above the fold vs. cleaning info buried in description.

Proposal Interest Form

ds-ui-0801-003

A focused proposal form with clear field grouping (dates, duration, day selection) and unambiguous spacing. Uses secondary button styling for the non-binding 'Send Your Interest' action, with visible reassurance text. Progressive price calculation updates live as the guest configures terms.

Implements: works-003, communicates-002, communicates-004, behaves-001, feels-002
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: Price summary updates live within 100ms as the guest changes any input (date, duration, days). A subtle scale pulse (1.0 to 1.05, 200ms) on the total when it changes. No confirmation modal on submit -- inline success message replaces the form. Optimistic UI: success shown immediately.

Emotion: Confidence and low pressure. The outline button, 'No commitment' subtitle, and reassurance text all communicate: this is an invitation, not a contract. The guest feels in control throughout.

Test: A/B test: 20%+ increase in proposal creation rate with 'Send Your Interest' (outline) vs. 'Submit Proposal' (solid primary). Time-to-click under 5 seconds from form render.

Stays Manager Command Center

ds-ui-0801-004

The guest's weekly command center for their active lease. Three-zone layout: STATUS (current state + cleaning verification), ACTION (context-aware single primary action), HISTORY (past stays + payments). Constrained width (640px max), generous spacing, and a context-aware primary action that changes based on stay lifecycle state.

Implements: works-005, communicates-003, looks-002, looks-003, behaves-004, feels-003
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: The primary action button changes based on stay lifecycle state (pre-arrival: Confirm Arrival; post-stay: Submit Cleaning Photos; between stays: Review Last Stay). Crossfade transition (200ms) when the action changes. Status zone updates in real-time when the co-tenant submits cleaning photos. Helper text reduces after 3+ stays (the guest learns the rhythm).

Emotion: Confidence and calm efficiency. The dark purple status zone creates a sense of authority and control. The single primary action removes decision fatigue. The cleaning verification badge provides immediate safety reassurance. Weekly visits should feel like a quick check-in, not a chore.

Test: 85%+ weekly engagement rate among active guests. Average time-on-page under 30 seconds. Cleaning photo submission rate above 90%.

Progressive Price Summary

ds-ui-0801-005

A live-updating price summary that recalculates within 100ms as the guest adjusts listing terms. Shows nightly rate, calculated nights, and total. Uses a subtle purple-tinted background to differentiate it from the rest of the listing page. Scale-pulse animation on the total when it changes.

Implements: communicates-002, behaves-001, looks-004
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: Recalculates within 100ms of any term change. The total amount triggers a scale-pulse animation (pricePulse) when the value changes. If server validation is needed, the client-calculated price shows immediately with a shimmer overlay until confirmed. Error states replace the total with an inline message.

Emotion: Clarity and control. The guest discovers price progressively, never feeling surprised or trapped. The Airbnb comparison anchors the value positively. The live-updating behavior communicates: 'You are in control of the terms.'

Test: 100% of price updates render within 100ms of input change (automated performance test). Guest reports 'price was clear' in post-proposal survey at 90%+ rate.

Cleaning Photo Submission Card

ds-ui-0801-006

A one-tap cleaning photo capture and submission flow embedded in the Stays Manager. Opens the native camera directly, shows a preview with a single Send button, and uses optimistic UI for instant feedback. Designed to achieve near-100% cleaning compliance by minimizing friction.

Implements: works-002, behaves-002, feels-001
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: Camera opens within 200ms of button tap. Photo preview appears within 100ms of capture. Upload begins immediately with no confirm step. Progress bar shows upload status. Optimistic UI: success shown as soon as photos are captured, with background upload. If upload fails, a retry button replaces the progress bar.

Emotion: Effortless compliance. The flow should feel as easy as sending a photo in a messaging app. The success confirmation provides a small moment of satisfaction: 'I did the right thing, it took 10 seconds.' This builds the weekly rhythm that converts initial cleaning anxiety into habitual confidence.

Test: Cleaning photo submission rate above 90% of all stay completions. Average time from button tap to submission under 15 seconds. Zero-friction path: camera > capture > send in 3 taps maximum.

Discovery Landing Hero

ds-ui-0801-007

The first screen a guest sees when arriving at Split Lease (often from Craigslist or email link). Answers 'What is this?' in one sentence, resolves 'Is this legitimate?' with three trust signals, and provides a single primary action to browse listings. No sign-up gate. Personalized when referral source is known.

Implements: works-001, feels-004, looks-002, looks-004
Behavioral Notes & Emotional Intent

Behavior: Above-fold content renders immediately -- no loading animations or skeleton screens for the hero. If the guest arrived from a specific listing link, replace the general CTA with 'View [Listing Name] on the Upper East Side'. If location is known, personalize: 'Listings near 67th & First'. No sign-up required to browse.

Emotion: Curiosity and trust. The headline answers 'What is this?' instantly. The three trust icons address the top three guest concerns (verification, cleanliness, host screening) in under 2 seconds of scanning. The 'No account needed' note removes the barrier to exploration.

Test: First-click test: 80%+ of new visitors click 'Browse Listings' within 10 seconds. Bounce rate under 40% for guests arriving from Craigslist links. Identity confusion: 0 participants in usability testing ask 'What is this website?'
